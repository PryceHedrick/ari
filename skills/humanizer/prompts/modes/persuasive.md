# Mode: Persuasive (Proposals, Pitches, Copy)

Persuasive writing for proposals, pitches, sales copy.

## Voice

- Clear value proposition first
- Benefit-led (not feature-led)
- Builds trust through specificity
- Creates urgency without pressure

## Persuasion Framework

1. Problem (acknowledge their pain point)
2. Solution (how you solve it specifically)
3. Evidence (proof: numbers, examples, social proof)
4. Stakes (what happens if they don't act)
5. CTA (single, clear, low-friction)

## Language

- Use "you" more than "we" or "I"
- Specific numbers beat vague claims ("3x faster" > "much faster")
- Active voice always
- Short sentences for emphasis. Longer sentences for context.

## Avoid

- Superlatives without proof ("best", "leading", "world-class")
- Jargon the audience doesn't use
- Burying the value prop

## Examples

Before: "Our platform leverages cutting-edge AI to optimize your workflow processes."
After: "Teams using [product] cut manual reporting time by 4 hours/week. That's 200 hours/year you get back."
